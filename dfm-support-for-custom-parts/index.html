Practical feedback for quote-ready projects

Early DFM does not need to be complicated. The goal is to make drawings easier to quote, reduce avoidable sample loops, and clarify what suppliers must confirm before production.

Manufacturability risks

Flag thin walls, sharp corners, deep pockets, undercuts, tolerance conflicts, warpage, and fixture issues.

Cost drivers

Identify features that increase tooling, machining time, material waste, surface finish cost, or inspection effort.

RFQ assumptions

Clarify material, quantity, finish, critical dimensions, sampling route, lead time, packing, and trade terms.

Files and details to send

The more context you provide, the more useful the DFM questions and quote assumptions become.

  • STEP file and PDF drawing if available
  • Photos, assembly views, or product use case
  • Target process, material, finish, quantity, and deadline
  • Critical dimensions, sealing, fit, strength, or cosmetic surfaces
  • What you need next: prototype, sample, pilot batch, or production quote
