Prototype machining with production intent

CNC is often the fastest path for early hardware validation. We help avoid over-tight tolerances, unclear finish expectations, and quote gaps that slow down startup product development.

Material and finish

Plan aluminum grade, stainless type, anodizing, plating, brushing, blasting, polishing, or passivation.

Tolerance review

Separate critical dimensions from cosmetic or non-critical features to control machining cost.

Inspection support

Confirm threaded holes, fit surfaces, cosmetic faces, key dimensions, and packaging before shipment.

What makes a CNC RFQ quote-ready

Clear inputs let us compare supplier quotes by risk and lead time, not only by unit price.

  • STEP file plus PDF drawing for tolerances and notes
  • Material grade or performance requirement
  • Prototype quantity and future batch estimate
  • Surface finish, color, cosmetic requirements, and deburring standard
  • Critical dimensions, threads, inserts, and inspection expectations
