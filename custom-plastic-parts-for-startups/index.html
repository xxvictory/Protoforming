Plastic part support from idea to small batch

Early-stage plastic projects often fail because the design is quoted before manufacturability, material stability, tooling approach, and assembly details are clear. We help you slow down the risky parts and speed up the quote-ready parts.

DFM before tooling

Review wall thickness, draft, ribs, bosses, threads, inserts, sealing areas, and assembly features.

Material guidance

Compare cost, strength, heat, impact, UV, finish, recycled-content risk, and supply availability.

Small-batch route

Match your project to CNC plastic, 3D printed samples, soft tooling, or production tooling as needed.

What to send for a useful quote

A good RFQ should include the part function, target quantity, material idea, finish, drawings, and where the part will be used.

  • STEP, STL, IGES, or PDF drawing files
  • Prototype quantity and expected first small-batch quantity
  • Target material or required performance
  • Surface finish, color, tolerance, and assembly requirements
  • Target country, timeline, and any target price

Have a plastic part to validate?

Send the drawing, photo, or concept. We will help identify DFM questions and the right sampling route.
